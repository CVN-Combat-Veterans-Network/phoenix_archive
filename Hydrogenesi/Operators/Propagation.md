## ADVANCED NOTES

### Wave Types

**Transverse Waves:**
- Oscillation perpendicular to propagation
- Example: Electromagnetic waves, light

**Longitudinal Waves:**
- Oscillation parallel to propagation
- Example: Sound waves, pressure waves

**Mixed/Complex Waves:**
- Multiple oscillation modes
- Example: Seismic waves, quantum wavefunctions

### Dispersion Relations

**Non-dispersive:**
- ω = v·k (constant velocity)
- Wave packet maintains shape
- Example: Electromagnetic waves in vacuum

**Dispersive:**
- ω = ω(k) (frequency-dependent velocity)
- Wave packet spreads over time
- Example: Water waves, quantum particles

### Information Preservation

**Lossless Propagation:**
- Pattern perfectly preserved
- No attenuation or distortion
- Example: Quantum unitary evolution

**Lossy Propagation:**
- Pattern degraded during transit
- Attenuation, noise, decoherence
- Example: Signal in noisy channel

### Speed Limits

- **Causal Signals:** v ≤ c (relativistic limit)
- **Phase Velocity:** Can exceed c (no information transfer)
- **Group Velocity:** Information propagation speed
