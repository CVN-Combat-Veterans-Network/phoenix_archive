   - Define central point in space
   - Set initial conditions

2. **Propagate Radially**
   - Extend structure outward from center
   - Maintain isotropy (equal in all directions)
   - Follow r(t) expansion law

3. **Generate Field**
   - Populate space with structure
   - Establish field density ρ(r)
   - Stabilize at scale

### Output
- **Field:** Distributed spatial structure
- **Radius:** Final extent r_f
- **Density Profile:** ρ(r) function

## ADVANCED NOTES

### Expansion Laws

**Linear Expansion:**
- r(t) = r₀ + v·t
- Constant velocity
- Used in: Wave fronts

**Exponential Expansion:**
- r(t) = r₀·e^(Ht)
- Accelerating growth
- Used in: Cosmic inflation

**Power Law Expansion:**
- r(t) = r₀·t^n
- Variable acceleration
- Used in: Universe evolution

### Isotropic vs Anisotropic

**Isotropic Expansion:**
- Equal in all directions
- Maintains symmetry
- Example: Perfect spherical waves

**Anisotropic Expansion:**
- Directionally dependent
- Breaks symmetry
- Example: Elliptical galaxies, directed growth

### Field Density Profiles

Common distributions:
- **Uniform:** ρ(r) = ρ₀ (constant)
- **Inverse Square:** ρ(r) = ρ₀/r² (forces)
- **Exponential:** ρ(r) = ρ₀·e^(-r/λ) (decay)
- **Gaussian:** ρ(r) = ρ₀·e^(-r²/2σ²) (quantum)
