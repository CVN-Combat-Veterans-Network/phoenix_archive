## MECHANISM

### Phase 1: COMPRESSION

**Process:**
- Mass accumulates through gravitational attraction
- Density increases beyond critical threshold
- Potential energy builds
- System approaches ignition point

**Cosmological Examples:**
- Gas cloud collapse ‚Üí Star formation
- Matter accretion onto black hole ‚Üí AGN formation
- Proto-galactic collapse ‚Üí Galaxy formation

---

### Phase 2: IGNITION

**Process:**
- Critical density reached
- Energy release initiated
- Jets/winds/radiation emitted
- Material ejected at high velocity

**Cosmological Examples:**
- Nuclear fusion ignition (stars)
- AGN jet formation (black hole accretion)
- Supernova explosion (stellar death)

---

### Phase 3: REPLICATION

**Process:**
- Ejected material seeds new structures
- Pattern propagates to offspring
- Lineage extends: ROOT ‚Üí GEN-0 ‚Üí GEN-1 ‚Üí ...
- Multiple offspring inherit pattern with variation

**Cosmological Examples:**
- Stellar nurseries from supernovae
- Satellite galaxies from AGN jets
- New planetary systems from stellar wind

## ADVANCED NOTES

### Compression Factor

The `compression_factor` determines:
- How much mass participates in ignition
- Energy available for replication
- Number of possible offspring

**High compression (0.9-1.0):**
- More energy released
- Fewer but more massive offspring
- Example: Supernova ‚Üí Neutron star + nebula

**Low compression (0.3-0.6):**
- Less energy released
- More but smaller offspring
- Example: Stellar wind ‚Üí Multiple planets/asteroids

### Replication Factor

The `replication_factor` determines:
- How many offspring structures
- Mass distribution among them
- Lineage branching pattern

**High factor (5-10):**
- Many offspring
- Distributed resources
- Example: Stellar cluster formation

**Low factor (1-3):**
- Few offspring
- Concentrated resources
- Example: Binary star formation

### Pattern Preservation

AGN Replication preserves **pattern**, not exact copy:
- Offspring inherit structure type (star ‚Üí stars, galaxy ‚Üí galaxies)
- Variation in size, composition, evolution
- **This is evolution at cosmic scale**
