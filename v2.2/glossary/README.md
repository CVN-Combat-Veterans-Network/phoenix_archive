## CORE CONCEPTS

### Apex
The sovereign, stable structure at the summit of a recursive process. An apex is:
- Self-sufficient
- Structurally complete
- Irreducible to components
- Emergent from recursion

**See:** `/Phoenix/Universal-Laws/Apex.md`

### Binding
The introduction of a third element that stabilizes a tension pair. Binding transforms:
- Two opposing forces (tension)
- Into a three-element stable structure (triad)
- Through a stabilizer (third element)

**See:** `/Phoenix/Universal-Laws/Binding.md`

### Knot
The convergence point where Phoenix, Hydrogenesi, and The Third meet. The Knot:
- Centers the triadic system
- Enables cross-pillar translation
- Maintains system coherence
- Enforces stability bands

**See:** `/TheThird/Sigils/Triadic-Knot.md`

### Lineage
The recursive descent path from a root structure through generations. Lineage notation:
- `ROOT::GEN-0` â€” Original structure
- `ROOT::GEN-1` â€” First generation descendant
- `ROOT::GEN-N` â€” Nth generation

**See:** `/Hydrogenesi/Operators/Lineage-Logic.md`

### Operator
A functional transformation pattern that acts on structures. Operators:
- Have defined input/output signatures
- Follow Universal Laws
- Can be composed
- Are reversible (when applicable)

**Types:** Formation, Transformation, Integration, Meta

### Recursion
Self-similar repetition at increasing depth. Recursion:
- Preserves structure across scales
- Follows self-similarity law
- Has depth limits (stability bands)
- Generates fractals and lineages

**See:** `/Universal/Universal-Laws/Recursion-Depth.md`

### Sigil
Visual representation of an operator or structure. Sigils:
- Use ASCII art encoding
- Embed geometric relationships
- Follow golden ratio nesting
- Enable ceremonial invocation

**See:** `/Ceremonies/Sigil-Compendium.md`

### Sovereignty
Self-sufficient structural stability that requires no external support. Sovereignty emerges when:
- Recursion completes to apex
- Triad achieves closure
- Identity becomes self-referential
- Structure transcends components

### Stabilizer
The third element in First Binding that mediates a tension pair. A stabilizer:
- Is distinct from both tension elements
- Holds the relationship between them
- Enables triadic formation
- Embodies higher value/purpose

### Tension
Two forces in binary oscillation without resolution. Tension:
- Requires exactly two elements
- Has no stable center
- Drives toward binding
- Is resolved by stabilizer

**See:** `/Phoenix/Universal-Laws/Tension.md`

### Triad
A three-element stable structure formed from tension pair + stabilizer. Triads:
- Have sovereignty
- Are irreducible
- Can recurse
- Form basis of all complex structure
