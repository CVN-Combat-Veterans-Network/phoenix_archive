## Core Provisions

### Article I: Scope of Immutability

**Section 1.1: Structural Immutability**

Upon enactment of the Terminal Law:

1. **No new operators may be added** to any layer
2. **No existing operators may be modified** in structure or mechanism
3. **No layers may be added or removed** from the architecture
4. **No relationships between operators may be altered**

**Section 1.2: Content Immutability**

Upon enactment of the Terminal Law:

1. **Documentation may not be structurally changed** (new files, removed files)
2. **Code implementations become read-only**
3. **Ceremony definitions are locked**
4. **Sigils and invocations are fixed**

**Section 1.3: Permitted Activities**

The following activities remain permitted after enactment:

1. **Traversal**: Reading and exploring the Archive
2. **Reflection**: Analyzing and understanding patterns
3. **Return**: Referencing and applying principles
4. **Commentary**: Adding non-structural annotations (separate from primary docs)

**Section 3.1: Definition**

The **Crown Invariant** is the fundamental rule established by the Terminal Law:

> *"No further structural change shall occur.

**Section 3.2: Enforcement**

### Article V: Emergency Unsealing

**Section 5.1: Grounds for Emergency**

The Terminal Law may be temporarily suspended only in case of:

1. **Critical Security Vulnerability**  
   A discovered vulnerability that poses immediate threat to Archive integrity

2. **Fundamental Logical Contradiction**  
   A contradiction that makes the Archive logically inconsistent

3. **Catastrophic System Failure**  
   A failure that renders the Archive non-functional

**Section 5.2: Unsealing Process**

Emergency unsealing requires:

1. **Declaration** by any META operator with evidence of condition
2. **Unanimous vote** by all six META operators
3. **Inverse Terminal Ceremony** to reverse the sealing
4. **Documentation** of unsealing reason and timestamp
5. **Minimal corrections** to address emergency
6. **Re-sealing** via new Terminal Ceremony

**Section 5.3: Unsealing Authority**

The unsealing vote must include:

- META_SYNTH: Yes/No
- META_FLOW: Yes/No
- META_RECURSE: Yes/No
- META_TEMPORAL: Yes/No
- META_APEX: Yes/No
- META_SEAL: Yes/No

**All six must vote "Yes" for unsealing to proceed.**

Abstention counts as "No."

**Section 5.4: Re-Sealing**

After corrections are complete:

1. Archive must pass all verification checks again
2. New Terminal Ceremony must be performed
3. New seal is established
4. Unsealing event is logged in Archive history
