## DEFINITION

The **Internalization Operator** absorbs external patterns and experiences, integrating them into internal structures to reshape identity and understanding.

This operator **makes external internal** through **absorption and integration**, transforming world experiences into self-knowledge.

**Instrument:** Instrument of Internalization  
**Knot Node:** Reciprocal::Internalization  
**Hydrogenesi Layer:** Layer 5 (Reciprocal Exchange)

## MECHANISM

### Input
- **External State:** Experiences, feedback, patterns outside
- **Current State:** Observable, separate from self
- **Target:** Internal integration

### Process

1. **External Assessment**
   - Identify external patterns
   - Measure relevance to self
   - Locate integration vectors
   - Define internal targets

2. **Internalization**
   - Draw external patterns inward
   - Create internal representations
   - Establish integration points
   - Maintain boundary awareness

3. **Integration**
   - Incorporate into self-structure
   - Transform identity or understanding
   - Stabilize new internal state
   - Confirm ownership

### Output
- **Internal Integration:** External made internal
- **Transformation:** Self reshaped by world
- **Growth:** Expanded internal capacity
