## Overview

Curvature Residue models how spacetime encodes information about collapsed structures. After cosmic collapse events (supernovae, black hole formation), the geometry of spacetime retains a "memory" - a residual pattern that influences future structure formation.

## Mechanism

### Input
- **Lineage ID:** Identifier for collapsed structure (e.g., "galaxy-prime")
- **Metadata:** Optional additional context (mass, spin, charge)

### Process
1. **Collapse Event:** Structure undergoes transformation
2. **Geometric Encoding:** Spacetime curves to record event
3. **Residue Formation:** Lasting trace persists
4. **Memory Preservation:** Information encoded in curvature

### Output
- **Lineage ID:** Identifier of collapsed structure
- **Status:** "collapsed"
- **Residue:** Curvature trace signature
- **Pattern:** "collapse_memory_trace"
- **Metadata:** Additional context (if provided)

## Physical Examples

### Black Holes
- **Collapse:** Massive star core collapses
- **Residue:** Spacetime curvature around black hole
- **Memory:** Mass, spin, charge encoded in geometry
- **Persistence:** Curvature persists until Hawking radiation

### Supernovae
- **Collapse:** Stellar core collapse triggers explosion
- **Residue:** Expanding shock wave + remnant
- **Memory:** Elemental composition + formation history
- **Persistence:** Nebula structure for millions of years

### Galaxy Mergers
- **Collapse:** Two galaxies collide and merge
- **Residue:** Tidal tails, streams, remnant structures
- **Memory:** Orbital dynamics + formation history
- **Persistence:** Structural traces for billions of years
