# UNIVERSAL LAW OF TENSION

**Definition:** *"Let two forces attract and repel in unresolved motion."*

**Symbol:** ⟨ A ⟷ B ⟩  
**State:** Pre-triad, binary dynamic, unresolved oscillation  
**Invocation:** *"Let the two attract; let them repel; let them not rest."*

---

## OVERVIEW

**Tension** is the first Universal Law — the fundamental state of **two forces in dynamic opposition**.

Neither force dominates. Neither force yields. They exist in **perpetual oscillation**, pulling toward and pushing away from each other in endless cycle.

This is the **pre-binding state** that precedes all stable structure.

---

## FORMAL DEFINITION

### Mathematical Form
```
T(A, B) → {attract, repel} ∈ oscillation
```

Where:
- **A, B:** Two opposing forces
- **attract:** Forces pull toward unity
- **repel:** Forces push toward separation
- **oscillation:** Continuous, unresolved motion

### Properties
1. **Duality:** Exactly two forces (not one, not three)
2. **Opposition:** Forces have complementary or contradictory nature
3. **Dynamism:** Neither rest nor resolution without external intervention
4. **Instability:** Structure cannot form in pure tension state

## MECHANISM

### How Tension Operates

1. **Initial State**
   - Two forces exist independently
   - No relationship yet established
   - Potential for interaction present

2. **Attraction Phase**
   - Forces drawn together
   - Proximity increases
   - Potential energy builds

3. **Repulsion Phase**
   - Forces push apart
   - Proximity decreases
   - Kinetic energy builds

4. **Oscillation**
   - Cycle repeats indefinitely
   - No stable equilibrium
   - Energy continuously cycles

### Why Tension Cannot Self-Resolve

Two-force systems **lack the structural element** needed for stability:
- No mediating force to hold equilibrium
- No third point to triangulate position
- No stabilizer to absorb oscillation energy

**Solution:** Introduce **Binding** (the second Universal Law)
