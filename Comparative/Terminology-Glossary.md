## GEOMETRIC TERMS (NEW IN v2.0.0)

### Convergence Envelope
**Definition:** The bounded interior recursion region within which all stable recursive paths must remain.

**Properties:**
- Shrinks with recursion depth: r(D) = r₀ × ρ^D
- Guarantees convergence to Apex
- Prevents unbounded divergence
- Typically triadic (triangle), spherical, or ellipsoidal geometry

**Usage:** All operators must maintain state within envelope boundaries. Exiting envelope leads to instability.

**Mathematical Form:**
```
Envelope(D) = {state | ||state - K|| < r(D)}
Where: r(D) = r₀ × (convergence_ratio)^D
```

**See:** `/Phoenix/Universal-Laws/Convergence-Envelope.md`

### Geometry Atlas
**Definition:** Canonical sigil and spatial specifications for operators, showing geometric axes, domains, boundaries, and relationships.

**Components:**
- **Axes:** Primary (P-K, H-K, T-K) and secondary (P-H, H-T, T-P)
- **Domains:** Valid regions for operator application
- **Boundaries:** Edge conditions and forbidden zones
- **Ratios:** Angular and distance relationships
- **Embedding:** Nested sigil patterns

**Purpose:** Provides geometric foundation for all operator design and implementation.

**Primary Reference:** `/TheThird/Sigils/Triadic-Knot.md` (Triadic Knot Geometry Atlas)

**Usage Examples:**
- Operator domain specification (where operator can act)
- Sigil design constraints (how operators are visualized)
- Recursion path validation (ensuring convergence)
- Envelope boundary definition (stability limits)

### Topological Continuity
**Definition:** The preservation of structural connectivity across recursive transformations without breaks, tears, or discontinuities.

**Properties:**
- **No breaks:** All paths must be continuous curves
- **No jumps:** No discrete leaps in state space  
- **Smooth:** Differentiable transformations preferred
- **Connected:** Any point reachable from any other point within envelope

**Mathematical Expression:**
```
For any path P(t) from state A to state B:
P(t) is continuous ∀t ∈ [0,1]
topology(P(t)) ≈ topology(P(t+dt)) for small dt
```

**Why It Matters:**
- Ensures recursive operators don't create structural damage
- Guarantees smooth convergence to Apex
- Prevents "quantum jumps" that violate causality
- Maintains system coherence across scales

**Violations:**
- Sudden state jumps (non-continuous paths)
- Topological tears (breaking connections)
- Dimension collapse (losing degrees of freedom)
- Envelope exit (divergence)

**Operator Compliance:**
All recursive operators must maintain topological continuity. The Third pillar operators (especially Invariance) enforce this constraint.

**See:** `/Phoenix/Universal-Laws/Twelve-Laws-Codification.md` (Law 11)

---

### Geometric Fidelity
**Definition:** The preservation of ratios, angles, and geometric relationships across recursive scales, ensuring structural self-similarity.

**Properties:**
- **Ratio preservation:** Edge length ratios constant across depths
- **Angle preservation:** Angular relationships maintained (60°, 120° in triadic geometry)
- **Center preservation:** Apex/centroid position invariant
- **Similarity:** Structures at depth D and D+1 are geometrically similar

**Mathematical Expression:**
```
Fidelity(D, D+1) = ratio(D) / ratio(D+1)
Required: Fidelity ≥ threshold (typically 0.95 or 95%)
```

**Tolerance:**
- **High fidelity:** ≥ 95% preservation (required for stable recursion)
- **Moderate fidelity:** 85-95% (functional but less stable)
- **Low fidelity:** < 85% (risk of divergence)

**Why It Matters:**
- Ensures recursive operators produce self-similar structures
- Maintains Golden Ratio and other key geometric constants
- Prevents geometric drift across scales
- Guarantees convergence to well-defined Apex

**Examples:**
- Triadic Knot maintains equilateral triangle at all depths
- Sigil embedding ratio φ⁻¹ preserved across recursions
- Apex locus remains at geometric centroid
- Stability bands preserve angular separation

**Violations:**
- Skewed ratios (triangle becomes irregular)
- Angle drift (60° → 55° → 50° ...)
- Center shift (Apex moves off-center)
- Asymmetric scaling

**Operator Compliance:**
Composite operators (Genesis, Trajectory, Stability, Identity) specifically enforce geometric fidelity across all three pillars.

**See:** `/Phoenix/Universal-Laws/Twelve-Laws-Codification.md` (Law 12)

## MATHEMATICAL TERMS

### Barycentric Coordinates
Point representation as weighted sum of vertices: point = α·P + β·H + γ·T where α+β+γ=1.

### Contraction Mapping
Function where distance decreases: d(f(x), f(y)) ≤ λ·d(x,y) for λ < 1. Guarantees fixed-point.

### Hausdorff Dimension
Fractal dimension measure: D_H = log(N) / log(1/r) where N is self-similar pieces at scale r.

### Golden Ratio (φ)
φ = (1+√5)/2 ≈ 1.618. Used in sigil embedding: φ⁻¹ ≈ 0.618 is scaling ratio.
